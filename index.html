Ginger-Soy Glazed Salmon

with Broccoli Rabe and Soba Noodles

Makes:2 Servings
Calories:About 625 Per Serving

For this recipe, you'll use flavorful decadent black bean paste. But not the black beans you're thinking of! This paste is made from soybeans that are salted and fermented then pureed with garlic. Black soybeans are one of the oldest known foods made from soybeans. Here, they form the base of a tangy, zesty glaze for salmon.

Wine Pairings:
Cabernet Sauvignon, Malbec, Nebbliolo, Red Burgundy

1 Prepare the Ingredients

Wash and dry the fresh produce. Heat a medium pot of salted water to boiling on high. Roughly chop the broccoli rabe, seperating the stems and leaves. Remove the salmon from the refridgerator to bring to room temperature. Peel and mince the ginger and garlic. Thinly slice the scallions on an angle, seperating the white and green parts. Pick the cilantro leaves off the stems. Using a peeler, remove the green rind of the lime, avoiding the white pith; mince the peel to get two teaspoons of lime zest. Quarter the lime.
